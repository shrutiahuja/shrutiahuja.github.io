## Objective: 
To develop a simple, low-cost paper-fluidic workflow that performs DNA isolation from urine using cellulose dipsticks and enables visual detection of PCR amplicons on an in-house nucleic-acid lateral flow strip for urinary tract infection (UTI) diagnostics.

## Background: 
UTIs affect millions worldwide and rapid identification of the causative organism is important for targeted therapy. Lateral flow assays are widely used for point-of-care testing but typically require upstream nucleic-acid purification and amplification. Paper-based dipstick extraction has been reported for several sample types but its application to urine and integration with a NALF readout for UTI targets has not been explored.

## Conclusion: 
A paper-fluidic pipeline combining cellulose dipstick extraction and a NALF readout can recover and detect E. coli DNA from urine with minimal equipment.
